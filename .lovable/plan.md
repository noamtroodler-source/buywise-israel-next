

# Rent vs Buy Guide — Real Value Overhaul

## What's Wrong Now
- "Why It Feels Loaded" has 4 cards but 2 repeat content from "How Renting Works" below
- No actual numbers comparing rent vs buy financially
- No lifestyle/practical reasoning — just structural descriptions
- "How Buying Changes Things" is vague, no concrete cost examples
- Missing the key insight: *when* each option makes more sense based on real variables

## New Structure (8 sections)

### 1. Overview (keep, minor tweak)
Update the "One-Sentence Reality" to be punchier: frame it as "this is a financial *and* life decision — here's how to think through both."

### 2. Why It Feels Loaded (trim to 2 cards)
Keep only **Housing Scarcity** and **Cultural Ownership Pressure**. Drop the other two (covered in renting section).

### 3. NEW — "The Numbers: What Renting vs Buying Actually Costs"
A side-by-side comparison section with real figures:
- **Example scenario**: ₪2.5M apartment in central Israel
  - **Buying**: Down payment ₪625K (25%), mortgage ₪1.875M → ~₪10,500/month at 5.25% over 25 years. Plus upfront: ~₪50K purchase tax (first-time) or ~₪200K (foreign), ~₪15-25K lawyer, ~₪25-50K renovation/appliances. Total year-one outlay: ₪700K-900K+
  - **Renting equivalent**: Same apartment rents for ~₪7,000-9,000/month. Year-one cost: ₪84K-108K + deposit. That ₪625K down payment invested conservatively earns ₪25-35K/year.
- **Key insight callout**: "In many cases, renting the same apartment you'd buy costs 30-40% less per month than the mortgage payment — and you keep your capital liquid."
- Note: "These are illustrative ranges, not guarantees. Use our calculators for your specific situation."

### 4. NEW — "The Practical Case for Renting First"
4 cards with real, specific advice:
- **More space for your budget** — "A ₪2.5M purchase in Tel Aviv buys ~60sqm. That same ₪8,000/month in rent gets you 80-90sqm in the same area, or 100+ sqm one neighborhood over."
- **Learn your city before committing** — "Neighborhoods change character block by block. What feels right on a pilot trip may not match daily life. Moving between rentals costs ₪5-10K. Buying the wrong neighborhood costs ₪100K+ in taxes and fees to undo."
- **Aliyah adjustment buffer** — "Job changes, ulpan schedules, kids' school placement, social circles — your first 1-2 years involve major life variables. A mortgage adds ₪10K+/month in fixed obligations during the most uncertain period."
- **Test before you commit** — "A full year of rent in a good apartment costs less than the purchase tax alone on most properties. That's a low-cost way to confirm you're in the right place."

### 5. How Renting Works (keep as-is — strong section)

### 6. NEW — "When Buying Starts to Make Sense"
4 cards presenting buying conditions neutrally (not pushing, just defining when the math and life factors align):
- **You've lived here 2+ years and know your area** — "You've experienced the neighborhood in summer and winter, know the school options, and aren't guessing about commute times."
- **Your income is stable and primarily in shekels** — "If you earn abroad, every mortgage payment carries currency risk. A 10% shekel appreciation means your mortgage effectively costs 10% more in your home currency."
- **You can cover all upfront costs without draining reserves** — "Down payment + purchase tax + lawyer + moving/renovation. If this requires emptying your savings, you're one emergency away from financial stress."
- **You plan to stay at least 7 years** — "Purchase tax + selling costs + capital gains tax mean you typically need 5-7 years of ownership just to break even vs renting. Below that, you're likely losing money."

### 7. How Buying Changes Things (sharpen with numbers)
Update the 4 existing cards with concrete figures:
- **Currency & CPI Risk**: Add "A CPI-linked mortgage track that starts at ₪5,000/month can rise to ₪6,500/month over 10 years if inflation averages 3%."
- **Purchase Tax Hit**: Add "On a ₪2.5M apartment: first-time buyer pays ~₪20K. Foreign buyer pays ~₪200K. That's a ₪180K difference."

- **Vaad Bayit and Arnona**: Add "Typical Arnona: ₪3,000-8,000/year depending on city and size. Vaad Bayit: ₪200-800/month depending on building age and amenities."
